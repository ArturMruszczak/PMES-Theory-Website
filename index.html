1. Core Ontology: The Dual-Space Geometry

Reality emerges from a 12-dimensional manifold M, formed by the product of two coupled 6-dimensional spaces:

Source Space (B – "Mother"):

B 6D space with signature (-, -, -, +, +, +).

  • Coordinates: ξ = (τ₁ᴮ, τ₂ᴮ, τ₃ᴮ, w₁, w₂, w₃)
  • Metric: ds_B² = -d(τ₁ᴮ)² - d(τ₂ᴮ)² - d(τ₃ᴮ)² + dw₁² + dw₂² + dw₃²

Derived Space (A – "Daughter"):

A physical 6D space arising from B via a phase transition (parabolic rotation).

  • Coordinates: x = (x₁, x₂, x₃, τ₁ᴬ, τ₂ᴬ, τ₃ᴬ)
  • Relation to B: The geometric dimensions are inherited (xᵢ = wᵢ), while the temporal dimensions are transformed (τⱼᴬ = fⱼ(τ₁ᴮ, τ₂ᴮ, τ₃ᴮ)).

Observed 4D Spacetime (A_obs):

An effective, low-energy projection of the full 6D space A.

  • At low energies, two time dimensions (τ₂ᴬ, τ₃ᴬ) are "compressed" or hidden.
  • We perceive one linear combination: t = (τ₁ᴬ + ατ₂ᴬ + βτ₃ᴬ)/N.
  • This compression is energetic; at high velocities/energies, these dimensions "unwind," explaining effects like time dilation.

2. The Fundamental Field and Photon-Membranes

Everything is an excitation of a fundamental field Ψ living in the full 12D space M.

Photon as an 11D Membrane-Soliton:

A photon is not a point particle. It is an 11-dimensional solitonic "membrane" that stretches across both spaces A and B.

  • It is localized in A but extended in 5 of the 6 dimensions of B.
  • Its oscillations in A are coupled to boundary conditions in B, creating resonances.
  • Particle masses (like the pion m_π and electron m_e) are identified as the energies of these fundamental resonances.

Key Scales & Calculated Constants:

  • λ_Ψ = ħ/(m_π c) ≈ 1.5×10⁻¹⁵ m (pion Compton wavelength / hadronic scale).
  • Fine-Structure Constant: α = π R_A² / (12 λ_{Ce}²) ≈ 1/137.036 (where λ_{Ce} is the electron Compton wavelength).
  • Gravitational Constant G: Emerges statistically. Its observed value is matched when considering 10 effective dimensions (6 from B + 4 active from A), with two time dimensions in A being inactive. Crucially, G is predicted to be dynamic, depending on the energy scale and the degree of "unwinding" of compactified time dimensions.

3. Emergent Phenomena and Radical Predictions

Emergent Gravity:

Gravity is not a fundamental force in A. It emerges in A as a statistical effect of interactions between Ψ excitations. The Einstein field equations are derived from a principle of extreme probability for field configurations.

Variable Gravitational Constant:

The model predicts that G decreases with increasing energy. This leads to testable consequences:

  • High-energy photons should experience weaker gravitational lensing.
  • Einstein rings should be energy-dependent and blurred.
  • In the early, high-energy universe, a smaller G could naturally drive inflationary expansion.

Other Key Predictions:

  • Resolution of Singularities: The central point of a black hole is not a mathematical singularity but a physical, topological knot of the Ψ field with a minimum size on the order of the Planck length (~10⁻³⁵ m).
  • Geometric Pauli Exclusion: A form of exclusion principle arises from the geometry of soliton overlap in space A, providing a fundamental basis for quantum statistics.
  • Photon Catastrophe Limit: There is a minimum time separation between photons (Δt_min ~ 3×10⁻²⁹ s), corresponding to the Planck energy scale, implying a fundamental discretization or limit to information density.

5. Philosophical Summary: A Cohesive Vision

PMES presents a coherent ontology:

  • Ψ is fundamental. Spacetime, matter, and forces are emergent excitations.
  • Space B is mathematically real but physically inaccessible, serving as the source geometry.
  • Space A is the full 6D physical reality, of which we ordinarily perceive only a 4D projection.
  • Photons are the "glue"—solitonic membranes connecting the two spaces and facilitating all interaction.
  • Mass, charge, and spin are quantum numbers arising from topological and resonant properties of Ψ configurations in A.
  • Dark Energy may be interpreted as the energy of the dynamic coupling between spaces A and B.
